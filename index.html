PLANS AND PROGRESS

Our idea is called Gotcha, and it will be easy for users to remember because it will make an impression on them and draw them in. In general, it contains the same features as other Lost and Found apps, such as a filter range, the main page where you can see all of the posts, and contact options. The difference between our Lost and Found app and others like Reclaimhub or MissingX is that it does not follow the standard Lost and Found app format. GOTCHA is unique in that it places a high value on the privacy, security, and experience of its users. Users will first need to register and enter personal information such as their name, age, and other facts. All data transmissions, for example, are normally encrypted after a user logs onto the GOTCHA app. Other systems won't be able to see or record the data you've sent them. After logging into the app, they will be taken to the main page, where they will be able to see all of the unclaimed lost and found things, as well as the item's name, who reported it, and when it was reported, as well as a picture of the item. They can also discover more information regarding the item, such as a thorough description and who to contact to claim it, when they click on the report. The user can either start a team discussion with the individual by clicking on the chatbox, or they can write the person an email asking for further information and claiming the item.

On the other hand, when a reporter checks in to the app on their own, they will see a "my item" tab, which will be divided into claimed and unclaimed items. It will show the reporter the objects that have previously been claimed by someone else, and it will show the goods that are still waiting to be claimed by someone else. For example, if the owner contacts the reporter and states, "This is my thing," and then claims it. The reporter can then select "mark as a claimed" and enter the claimed person's name to find out who claimed it; however, the reporter can also provide some notes in case there are any issues.

Furthermore, there will be a posting page for the user to post what they found or lost during the posting process. If a person wants to report something they've lost, there will be sections for them to input the item's name and description, as well as attach a photo. If the user wishes to report what they've found, there are certain questions relating to the objects, such as a category and a description box where the user can write where they found it, the appearance, a photo, and the item's current quality. The item's details will appear on the app's home screen after it has been posted. Users can quickly locate something they've misplaced by using the "filter by category" feature on the home screen. For example, if the user is looking for their phone, they can use the “devices” option to filter the list of objects, and there will be a reset icon for them to click if they want to reset the filter.
